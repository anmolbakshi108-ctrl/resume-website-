About Me

Growing up, I was always the kind of person who asked too many questions why does this work the way it does, what if we tried it differently, who actually benefits from this. That curiosity never really went away. If anything, it's only gotten louder.

Entrepreneurship pulled me in because it's one of the few spaces where that restlessness actually becomes useful. The process of identifying a gap, thinking through how to fill it, and building something with your own hands there's nothing quite like it. It's messy and uncomfortable and I love every bit of it.

Artificial Intelligence is the other thing I can't stop thinking about. Not because it's trendy, but because it genuinely feels like one of those rare moments in history where everything is shifting at once. The way businesses are run, the way decisions get made, the skills that actually matter all of it is changing. I want to understand that shift deeply, not just observe it from the outside.

Beyond the big ideas though, I think what shapes you more than anything is how you show up in the small moments. I try to lead with empathy to actually listen before I speak. I value resilience, not the performative kind, but the quiet ability to reset after things don't go to plan. And I genuinely believe that collaboration beats solo hustle almost every time.

I'm a student in the truest sense still learning, still evolving, still getting things wrong. But I'm paying attention, and I'm building with purpose.
